AidBriefBench is an AI decision brief tool for small nonprofit teams working on field operations.

The problem is simple: volunteer coordinators often work from messy notes, chat threads, and last-minute constraints. In this demo, the scenario is a food pantry that needs to coordinate drivers, pickup sites, dropoff locations, and cold storage limits during a rush window.

I click Load sample to bring in the rough field notes. The notes include driver availability, phone constraints, privacy concerns, and a Raspberry Pi available at the pantry office.

Then I click Analyze. The app asks Gemma 4 to turn the messy context into structured JSON, not a generic chatbot answer. That JSON becomes a decision brief with a summary, model rationale, risks, build plan, user experience notes, and assumptions.

For social-good workflows, the most important parts are the risks and assumptions. AidBriefBench keeps those visible so a human coordinator can review the plan before acting.

This aligns with SDG 2, Zero Hunger, by supporting food pantry logistics. It also supports small community organizations that need practical AI tools without heavy infrastructure.

The app is deployed, the source code is public, and judges can try it immediately in demo mode. With a real Gemma 4 API key or local Gemma runtime, the same workflow can become a privacy-aware planning assistant for nonprofit teams.
